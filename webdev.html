Introduction.

Fitness is My Forte is a website created for Jennifer RH, a local personal fitness trainer with no previous website or brand identity. This was a client capstone project for a design and development program I took.

Business goals:

1. A dynamic, easy to maintain site where information about her services could be easily discovered, updated, and understood.

2. Allow clients to contact Jennifer directly (by program guidelines, we were not liable to set up an e-commerce website)

The primary goal for Jennifer's site was to display her services, and offer a way of contact for clients to reach her. Knowing this, we began with a content-first approach in creating her product. This directive guided our information architecture.

Before development, we confirmed with the client with what aesthetics/design features she would like in her product. Looking back, I wish we could have spent more time on design so that her brand was more modern and cohesive. In addition, I think we could have been more assertive in suggesting recommendations, as we ended with colours that did not necessary work with the black and grey colour palette.

After the IA and design guide was finalized, we ran through several iterations of the website layout. Code development took up the bulk of the project time as we were starting from a blank Wordpress template. One lesson learned was to start from mobile-first, because we had to fix a lot of bugs for mobile/assets resizing.

Well, transferring the content from server to server was kind of really stressful, and took much longer than we had initially thought... but that sums up our one-month capstone project!

Lessons learned for next time: project management and a workflow framework to guide the team is extremely important. Also team briefs are so so important, and communication is key. Knowing what I know now, it would be extremely valuable to see what sort of analytics we can get from Jennifer's site and how to made changes that would better promote her brand/product!
