UX + UI Design

I decided to redo the 2019 Google UX Design Challenge as a way to see how my design thinking has improved. I am super grateful to have gotten the opportunity to play around with this design challenge, so let's see how we can create a fun experience for university students!

Investigation.

After conducting surveys and interviews, I've identified 4 key issues.

Goal:

Give new and returning students an easy way to to sign up and stay updated with school organizations. The chosen platform is a Progressive Web App (PWA).

Welcome.

Due to the flexibity of the PWA platform, students can be directed to this application either through an external link (eg. from the official school site or school emails) or via QR codes at school events and club boothing days. This removes the hassle of downloading an app that may not be used through out the school year.

To ensure safety and reduce misconduct, students are required to login through their official school student account, in which this application will be linked to. This would be a familiar process for both incoming and returning students.

Set up your account.

Once the student has logged in, they are able to confirm their name or change it. In addition, the student email address associated with the school login will automatically be inputed into clubs.day. This will be used to send confirmations & notifications later on.

At the inital onboarding, students can begin to customize their clubs.day account by selecting their interests. This page can also begin to introduce campus life, popular activities and so on. After this is completed, the PWA is ready to go.

Goal:

Display the content in a clean and concise format that is easy to understand and personalized for home, upcoming, groups and propose.

Many students indicated that information was hard to find and confusing on school websites. To make the experience easier and faster overall, I've simplified the design to a minimum to show relevant information for busy students.

Stay updated anytime.

RVSP event cards will be shown on the homepage feed, which redirected to the upcoming page (in detail) if the user choses to go to that event.

Students will be able to RVSP on this platform if they choose not to use another form of social media sign-up, and a confirmation email/itenerary can be sent to the user with the school email account associated to clubs.day.

Join some, create some.

Students can join clubs or star them for interest, which will help in creating the custom homepage feed and further recommendations through out the PWA. Clicking on the joined club will lead you back to the club page.

One of the key comments that stood out during interviews was the lack of information about the procedure of creating a club as well as collecting student signatures. If more time was given, I would focus on a design of collecting signatures easily, such as sending a support 👍 indicates that you support the start of the club and have given your signature via student email.

Further development: the web format

School administration and other more traditional office roles may be more familiar with working in a desktop platform. By creating a PWA, this can benefit the full spectrum of people who are comfortable with technology to those who are more old-schooled. For the desktop design, it is a readjustment of the mobile edition, with the search bar remaining static in the left panel and the bottom nav bar moved to a top nav position.

More further exploration, this design can consider integrating existing tools such as Google Calender or Facebook reviews, which would encourage students to stay involved knowing the club is active.

Other ideas for this design challenge included an AR directional guide (to the event/club space), look into a way of proposing clubs with ease (a club directory with past club history?) and an assistance chatbot for guiding students or making smart suggestions.

Reflections.

If I had more time to work on this project, I would have explored the perspectives of the other parties involved, such as school admins, club organizers and professors. For this redesign, I focused on the students' user experience using the PWA. In addition, I think I could have refined the UI. One potential way to personalize the UI is to match buttons, nav bars and CTA elements with palettes that are correlated to a school's brand colors (eg. blue for UBC).

However, the biggest challenge I believe that I could improve on is how to design for a PWA and how it would lay out dynamically between both the mobile and desktop version. During the process of creating some of the high-fid mockups, I realize that some of the elements are not optimized for PWAs and that some of wireframe designs are more alike to an app. Thanks for reading! ☻
