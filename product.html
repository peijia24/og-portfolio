Research & Design

Falls are a leading cause of injuries and deaths in seniors. In the states, 1 senior dies every 19 minutes as a result of a fall. (U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention)

One of my courses this year is New Venture Design and in this course, my team and I are creating a smart wearable insole that tracks balance and makes smart exercise recommendations for seniors.

Problem scope.

Seniors are an underrepresented and undercatered part of demographics in many countries. More specifically, the issue of senior health is highly reactive (eg. treating the problem once it has occured) versus preventative treatment. We noticed that many seniors and healthcare practioners only cared about the dangers of falls once a fall has occured, and at that stage - the senior individual has already suffered a loss in quality of life due to surgery, recovery, permanent damage, etc.

After over 100 interviews with seniors, healthcare providers and adult children, we have pinpointed these key features for our product.

Prototype V1.

To quickly build and break our idea, we decided to apply to nwHacks 2020 and create our first prototype. We are able to build a working model for both the hardware and software in less than 24 hours.

How it works.

The sensors in our smart sole collects the user's balance data. This data is sent to our algorithm and is interpreted into good or bad balance. The data is then sent back to our companion app and displayed to the user. Depending on the balance score, appropriate exercise recommendations will be made for the user.

Hardware.

Arduino UNO & Force-sensing Resistors.

Software.

Keras, Google App Engine, ReactNative, Javascript, HTML, Python, Time Series Analysis, Recurrent Neural Network.

I created the UX/UI design of our app and implemented them into ReactNative, which I have never used before. By the end of the hackathon, we had 3 screens which displayed the relevent information of our first app prototype.
